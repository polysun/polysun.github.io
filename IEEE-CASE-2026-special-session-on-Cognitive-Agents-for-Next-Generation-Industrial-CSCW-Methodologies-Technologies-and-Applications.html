Call for Papers

Special Session on Cognitive Agents for Next-Generation Industrial CSCW: Methodologies, Technologies, and Applications

2026 IEEE 22nd International Conference on Automation Science and Engineering

Open from Now to 1 March 2026 for submissions

Aim and Scope:

As Industry 4.0 evolves toward Industry 5.0, the paradigm of smart manufacturing and industrial services is shifting from automation-centric to human-centric. In these increasingly complex sociotechnical systems, human operators and engineers face unprecedented information complexity and cognitive load. Traditional Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW) systems often function as passive tools, lacking a deep understanding of human intent and semantic awareness of dynamic environments. Consequently, they struggle to meet the demands of flexible manufacturing, remote healthcare, and emergency response scenarios where seamless collaboration is critical.

Themes:

This special session aims to explore how Cognitive Agents (specifically those powered by Large Language Models (LLMs), Large Multimodal Models (LMMs), and Generative AI)can reshape Industrial CSCW. We envision these agents not merely as tools, but as active "teammates" capable of perceiving environmental context, interacting via natural language, performing causal reasoning, and ultimately assisting or autonomously executing tasks. This session will provide a primary forum for cross-industry multidisciplinary research, bridging Artificial Intelligence, Human Factors, Systems Engineering, and Robotics. Topics of Interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Architectures for Industrial Agents: Cognitive architecture design based on LLMs and Agentic Workflows
  • New Paradigms of HCI: Multi-modal interaction in industrial environments (Natural Language, Gesture, AR/VR)
  • Knowledge and Reasoning: Industrial Knowledge Graphs, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), and Neuro-symbolic reasoning for CSCW
  • Trust and Safety: Explainable AI (XAI), trust metrics, and ethical boundaries in cognitive collaboration
  • World Models and Spatial Intelligence: 3D perception, physical reasoning, and environment simulation for predictive industrial interaction
  • Embodied Collaboration: Embodied AI and Physical Human-Robot Interaction (pHRI)
  • Multi-Agent Systems: Human-Machine-AI teaming, task allocation, and co-evolutionary mechanisms
  • Smart Manufacturing Applications: Human-robot collaborative assembly, quality inspection, and generative design
  • Broad Industrial Applications: Cognitive collaboration in smart healthcare, logistics, and service robotics
