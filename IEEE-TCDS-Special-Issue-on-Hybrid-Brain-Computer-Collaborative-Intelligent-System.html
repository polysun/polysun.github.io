Call for Papers

Special Issue on Hybrid Brain-Computer Collaborative Intelligent System

IEEE Transactions on Cognitive and Developmental Systems

Open from Now to 30 November for submissions

Aim and Scope:

Brain-computer fusion is regarded as one of the most important technologies in the 21st century, and its research will promote the development and progress of information science, artificial intelligence and cognitive science. The fusion and even integration of biological intelligence (brain) and machine intelligence (machine), which perfectly combines the perceptual and cognitive ability of the brain with the computing power of the machine, is expected to produce a more powerful intelligence form -- hybrid intelligence, which is beyond the reach of existing biological intelligence systems and machine intelligence systems.

Achieving brain-computer fusion intelligence requires breakthroughs in some natural human-computer interaction technologies. Natural human-computer interaction technology mainly realizes the information interaction between man and machine with no difference. It focuses on the improvement of the operator's operation ability under complex tasks. This requires the acquisition of cognitive characteristics such as the operator's physiology, emotion, motivation and ability, and the building of a cognitive interaction model that can actively adapt to its perception channel (vision, hearing, touch) and behavior channel (speech, hand, foot, head, eyes, and physiology), so as to improve the operator's behavioral ability to handle complex tasks. It can be seen that the hybrid brain-computer collaborative intelligence system continuously improves the interaction level between operator and machine intelligence from operation interaction, knowledge interaction to cognitive interaction.

Themes:

The purpose of this special issue is to provide a forum for researchers to present the latest advances in cognitive analysis methods and applications from the perspective of brain-computer fusion and cognitive enhancement. The list of possible topics includes, but is not limited to:

  • Neurophysiological signals and functional images in the context of the task
  • Brain information representation and coding for perception-motor integration
  • Brain cognitive enhancement and feedback control
  • Visual/auditory/tactile enhanced hybrid intelligent system
