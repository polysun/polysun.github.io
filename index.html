Poly Z.H. Sun is currently pursuing his Ph.D. degree in Industrial Intelligent System with the Department of Industrial Engineering, School of Mechanical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China.

He has authored/co-authored over 30 research papers in refereed international journals and conferences. His current research interests include intelligent transportation systems, human factors in driving, neuroergonomics, brain and cognitive status under maneuvers, physiological parameter measurement, bio-inspired vision mechanism, operations research, and their applications in human-in-the-loop transportation systems. He is a member of the IEEE ITSS, IEEE CIS, IEEE SMCS, and the Association for Computing Machinery. He also has been severing as the reviewer for many top-tier international journals and conferences in his research field.

In addition, he participated the standardization of Industrial Internet Identification and Resolution. The standard he participated is promoting the development of manufacturing networking in China.

Since 2021, his research has shifted from Evolutionary Algorithms-based Operational Research to

  • Human-in-the-Loop Intelligent Vehicles: (1) Measurement of cognitive state and physiological parameters (eye movement, EEG, EMG, ECG, and respiration) during driving tasks; (2) Human factors and ergonomics under driving tasks (driving fatigue, driving comfort, driving competency); (3) Biology and human factors inspired intelligent vehicle driving safety assurance (collision detection, driving assistance system design); (4) Human-machine co-driving and human-computer interaction.
  • Human-in-the-Loop Intelligent Transportation Systems: (1) Human-oriented participatory traffic control (flow-regulated travel resource distribution); (2) Mechanism design of socialized crowdsourcing intelligent transportation platform (order distribution mechanism, incentive mechanism);(3) Operation, service, and management for human-centered intelligent transportation system and platform (shared travel, multimodal transport).
